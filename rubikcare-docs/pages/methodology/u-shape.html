DEFINITION

What is U-Shape?

U-Shape is the visual pathway of collapse at the bottom of the periodic table. Not a straight line — but the shape of the letter U: starting from deterioration of inputs on the right side, descending into a void in the middle, then rising — but not returning to what it was — ending in catastrophe on the left side.

Why U-Shape, not L-Shape or V-Shape?

L-Shape: collapse starts at one point and simply declines. V-Shape: collapse is sharp and then returns. But U-Shape has a double bottom. There is a "void" (absence) then "filling" (with corruption). The two stages together — void first, then poison — are what create the complete U shape.

U-Shape = Right Arm ↓ + Double Bottom (Void + Filling) + Left Arm ↑
Collapse begins at the edges → reaches the void → the void fills with poison

This is the conceptual innovation of U-Shape: collapse does not happen all at once. There are two separate stages, separated in time.

⚠️ The Real Danger: Stability

The void (C₁) is dangerous but unstable — it is possible to exit quickly. The swamp (C₂) is stable — it has its own system, its own heroes, and its own rewards. Exiting C₂ is much harder than exiting C₁. Therefore: the earlier you detect the void, the easier it is to save the system.

How to Detect You Are in U-Shape

VOID ENGINEERING

Why the Void Is an Active Force

The void in U-Shape is not passive. It is a gravitational force. In physics, nature abhors a vacuum — any empty space will attract whatever is around to fill it. In the market, the same is true: the absence of d‑Block does not mean "nothing" — it means that something else will fill this space.

Stages of Void Filling:

1. Shock: d‑Block disappears — a real temporary void. (Short duration)
2. Fragility: The system is weak but corruption has not yet appeared. (Medium duration)
3. Leakage: Small corruption factors begin at the edges — False Visits, undocumented discounts. (Medium-Long duration)
4. Settlement: Misdiagnosis settles in the center. (Long duration)
5. Completion: All f‑Block elements fill the void. (Stable — the Swamp)
