DEFINITION

What is Zone B?

Zone B occupies rows 3 to 6 of the periodic table. It is the grey zone where companies are not at the digital peak (Zone A) nor in complete collapse (Zone C). They have some digital tools, but integration is incomplete, data is partial, and outcomes are moderate.

Location in the Periodic Table

Zone B spans Rows 3, 4, 5, and 6 — four rows between Zone A (above) and Zone C (below). The lower you move within Zone B, the less digital maturity you find, and the wider the gaps become.

SUB‑ZONES

Upper Zone B vs. Lower Zone B

Zone B is not a single block — it consists of two distinct sub‑zones with different levels of digital maturity.

OTC/E‑Commerce and General RX. Digital tools exist and function reasonably well: B2B platforms, basic CRM, cash back, analytics. But integration is incomplete — e‑prescription may be missing, PHR is absent, CME is still partial. Outcomes are good but not excellent.

CHARACTERISTICS

What Defines Zone B?

📊
Partial Digital Tools
Some d‑Block elements exist — CRM (basic), B2B (partial), analytics (delayed). But the full suite of 21 digital elements is not active.
⚠️
Incomplete Integration
CRM does not connect to B2B. PSP is not linked to analytics. Data flows are broken, creating blind spots and inefficiencies.
🔍
Biased / Delayed Data
Data comes from intermediaries, not directly from the point of sale or patient. Forecasts are based on estimates, not real demand.
🎯
Moderate Outcomes
Patient adherence is 50‑75%. Diagnosis accuracy is good but not guaranteed. Prescription errors still occur due to paper handwriting.
REAL‑WORLD EXAMPLES

What Happens in Zone B?

Example 1: Mid‑Size Pharma Company — OTC & General RX

The company has a basic CRM that tracks rep visits, but physicians do not rate the visits. It has a B2B platform, but only 40% of pharmacies use it — the rest rely on traditional distributors with 50‑60% discounts. Cash back exists but is not automated — it requires manual monthly review. Outcome: Sales are acceptable, but margins are eroded, data is incomplete, and forecasting is unreliable.

Example 2: Specialty Pharma Company — Specialist RX & Institutional

The company has highly skilled specialist reps, but no CRM linking visits to actual prescription outcomes. CME is delivered through physical conferences — doctors travel and leave their clinics, incurring high costs. PSP is managed on paper — patients enroll, but no one knows whether they adhere to treatment. Outcome: Strong scientific reputation, but high operational costs, and patient outcome data is almost non‑existent.

The Opportunity in Zone B

Companies in Zone B are not "failures" — they are close to success. They have a foundation to build upon. The gap between Zone B and Zone A is not an abyss — it is 3‑4 digital elements waiting to be completed. This is precisely why Zone B represents the biggest opportunity for rapid transformation.

THE TRANSFORMATION PATH

How to Rise from Zone B to Zone A

Companies in Zone B already have a foundation. The journey to Zone A requires adding specific digital elements in a logical sequence.
⚡
Step 1 — Complete B2B
Move all pharmacies to the B2B platform. Eliminate intermediaries. Reduce discounts by 15‑25% and gain full pricing control. (3‑6 months)
🔷
Step 2 — Dual CRM
Add physician rating to the CRM. Detect false visits, gain accurate forecasting, and improve rep performance. (2‑4 months)
🎓
Step 3 — Digital CME
Replace physical conferences with a digital CME platform. Reduce costs by 60%, provide accredited points, and build ethical physician engagement. (4‑8 months)
📋
Step 4 — E‑Prescription + PHR
Implement digital prescriptions and personal health records. Eliminate handwriting errors and provide complete treatment history. (6‑12 months)
💊
Step 5 — Digital PSP
Launch a fully digital Patient Support Program with enrollment, dispensing tracking, adherence monitoring, and RWE generation. (3‑6 months)
🔗
Step 6 — Full Integration
Connect CRM + B2B + PSP + CME + Analytics into a single ecosystem. Achieve complete visibility and strategic decision‑making. (6‑12 months)

"Zone B is not failure. It is delay. The difference between the delayed and the successful is not in capabilities — but in sequencing. Start with the fastest‑impact element, then build upon it."
